1. Overview

Aumo is an iOS translation application for short, natural conversations.

The App allows users to:

  • translate recorded speech;
  • translate typed text;
  • translate between Thai, Russian, Spanish, and English;
  • configure translation direction, voice, tone, and certain regional settings;
  • request a new translation of the same source text;
  • store successful translations locally in History;
  • copy translated text;
  • submit optional categorical feedback about translations;
  • switch between system, light, and dark appearance modes; and
  • open the Privacy Policy and Terms of Use inside the App.

Most translation features require internet access.

2. Information the App Processes

The App does not create user accounts or personal profiles. It processes the following categories of information in order to function.

a. Recorded Audio

If you use voice translation, the App records audio after you grant microphone permission.

The audio is temporarily stored on your device and transmitted to Aumo’s backend for transcription and translation.

Audio is not saved in local History. The App normally removes temporary audio after cancellation, successful translation, unusable input, or completion of a retry flow. Temporary files may remain temporarily in the normal circumstances of an app crash, operating-system termination, or system-managed temporary storage.

The Aumo backend does not save raw audio in its database, Redis, or file storage. It forwards audio to OpenAI for transcription and may send it again if a transcription retry is required.

b. Typed Text

If you use typed translation, the text you submit is transmitted to Aumo’s backend for translation.

The text-entry field is not stored as a permanent draft. After a successful translation, cleaned source text and translated text may be saved locally in History.

c. Transcription, Cleaned Source Text, and Translation

The backend may create or return:

  • speech transcription;
  • cleaned or normalized source text;
  • detected language;
  • translated text; and
  • validation or style results.

This information is displayed in the App. Successful source/translation pairs are stored locally in History until deleted.

Ordinary successful translations are not written to Aumo’s feedback database merely because they were translated.

d. Language and Style Settings

The App processes and may transmit settings needed to produce a translation, including:

  • source and target language;
  • Their Voice and My Voice direction;
  • Speak as;
  • Tone;
  • Thai voice style; and
  • Spanish region.

These preferences are stored locally and may be included in translation requests.

e. Translation History

For each successful translation, the App may store locally:

  • a History entry identifier;
  • date and time;
  • cleaned source text;
  • translated text;
  • source and target language;
  • style settings;
  • speaker side; and
  • input mode.

History remains on your device until you delete an item, clear History, reset app data, or uninstall the App, subject to normal iOS behavior.

f. Translation Feedback

If you choose a feedback reason for an active translation, the App may send:

  • the selected feedback reason;
  • a translation identifier;
  • an encrypted feedback receipt;
  • an idempotency identifier;
  • an optional random anonymous tester identifier;
  • app version; and
  • app build.

The feedback request itself does not directly send raw audio or client-supplied source and translated text. Instead, the backend validates the encrypted receipt and derives the relevant translation snapshot from it.

When feedback is successfully accepted, Aumo may store:

  • original source text or transcription;
  • cleaned source text;
  • translated text;
  • input type;
  • source and target language;
  • voice, tone, style, and regional settings;
  • model, prompt, style-guide, backend, app, and schema version information;
  • the feedback reason;
  • an optional anonymous tester identifier;
  • a hashed App Attest-derived app-instance identifier;
  • timestamps; and
  • export status.

The App currently provides categorical feedback only. It does not provide a free-text feedback field or suggested-translation field.

Feedback is confirmed optimistically in the interface. A visual confirmation may appear before the backend request has completed and does not guarantee delivery.

g. Anonymous Tester Identifier

When feedback is submitted, the App may generate a random UUID and store it in the iOS Keychain. This identifier is used to associate feedback from the same installation or device context.

It is not an Apple ID, advertising identifier, email address, account ID, device name, or contact identifier.

The App does not currently provide a control to view, reset, or delete this identifier.

h. Security and App Attest Information

Aumo uses Apple App Attest and DeviceCheck to help verify legitimate app instances, protect the backend, prevent abuse, and enforce rate limits.

Depending on the flow, the App and backend may process:

  • App Attest key identifiers;
  • attestation objects;
  • assertions;
  • challenge identifiers;
  • client-data hashes;
  • an Aumo-generated app-instance identifier;
  • public-key and sign-counter records;
  • verification environment and timestamps; and
  • hashed identifiers used for rate limiting or feedback storage.

Raw App Attest values are not included in ordinary translation content or feedback exports.

i. Rate-Limit and Network Metadata

The backend uses counters and pseudonymous identifiers to enforce per-minute and daily request limits.

For verified traffic, rate-limit keys use a hash of the Aumo app-instance identifier. In certain development or fallback configurations, hashes of a bearer token and network IP information may be used.

Rate-limit counters expire after their fixed minute or UTC-day window. The App may receive limit, scope, wait time, and reset time when a request is blocked.

j. App and Interface Preferences

The App stores interface preferences locally, including:

  • selected languages;
  • voice, tone, and region preferences;
  • appearance preference; and
  • certain App Attest registration state.

k. Device Motion

The About screen may read device pitch and roll through CoreMotion to create a local parallax effect.

Motion values are used only to move artwork on screen. They are not stored, transmitted, logged, or used to infer location, health, or activity.

l. Clipboard

When you use Copy, translated text is written to the iOS system clipboard.

Clipboard retention and access are controlled by iOS. Aumo does not control whether another app reads clipboard content after you copy it.

3. How Information Is Stored

Aumo uses the following storage mechanisms.

a. UserDefaults

The App uses UserDefaults to store:

  • translation History;
  • language and style preferences;
  • appearance preference;
  • App Attest key identifier; and
  • App Attest registration-complete state.

b. Keychain

The App uses the iOS Keychain to store the optional random anonymous tester identifier used for feedback.

c. Temporary Files and Memory

The App uses temporary local files for recorded audio and memory-only state for active results, feedback receipts, retry contexts, idempotency keys, typed editor text, and motion offsets.

d. Redis

The backend uses Redis-compatible infrastructure to store:

  • short-lived App Attest challenges;
  • App Attest app-instance, key, public-key, sign-counter, and security records; and
  • short-lived rate-limit counters.

Challenges normally expire after approximately five minutes or are consumed after use. Rate-limit counters expire after the applicable fixed window plus a small buffer.

App Attest app-instance and key records do not currently have an automatic expiry in the backend implementation.

e. Neon PostgreSQL

Aumo uses Neon PostgreSQL to store submitted translation-feedback reports.

Ordinary translations that are not reported are not stored in this database.

Submitted feedback rows are not currently deleted automatically by the backend.

f. Local Operator Exports

Authorized operator tools may export new feedback reports to CSV and JSONL files for manual review.

Exports may contain source text, cleaned source text, translated text, language/style information, model and version metadata, anonymous tester identifier, app version/build, and feedback reason.

Exports exclude raw audio, raw App Attest identifiers, App Attest assertions, bearer tokens, IP addresses, database credentials, idempotency keys, request fingerprints, and receipt data.

The current code does not define an automatic retention or deletion schedule for exported files.

9. Data Retention

Current retention behavior is as follows.

  • Temporary audio on device: normally removed after cancellation, success, unusable input, or completion of retry handling; abnormal termination and OS temporary-storage behavior may affect timing.
  • Ordinary translation data on Aumo backend: processed transiently and not stored in the feedback database unless feedback is submitted.
  • Local History: retained until deleted by the user, cleared, reset, or removed with the App, subject to normal iOS behavior.
  • Feedback receipt: valid for approximately 24 hours and held by the client; it is not stored in a server-side receipt database.
  • App Attest challenges: normally retained for up to approximately five minutes and consumed after successful use.
  • Rate-limit counters: retained through the applicable minute or UTC-day window plus a small buffer.
  • App Attest app-instance and key records: currently have no automatic expiry in the backend implementation.
  • Submitted feedback reports: currently remain in Neon until manually deleted or a future retention process is introduced.
  • Exported feedback files: currently have no automatic deletion schedule.
  • Application and provider logs: retention is controlled by the relevant hosting or service provider and current account settings.

We may introduce or change retention periods as the App develops. Material changes will be reflected in an updated Privacy Policy.

10. Data Security

Aumo uses technical and organizational measures intended to protect information, including:

  • HTTPS-based service endpoints;
  • App Attest verification;
  • one-time security challenges;
  • signature and sign-counter validation;
  • hashed identifiers for rate limiting and feedback storage;
  • environment-variable secret management;
  • encrypted and authenticated feedback receipts using AES-256-GCM;
  • parameterized database queries;
  • idempotency and duplicate-report protection;
  • restricted operator-only feedback export tools;
  • no public database or export endpoint; and
  • content-minimized application logging.

No method of transmission, processing, or storage is completely secure. The Developer cannot guarantee absolute security.
